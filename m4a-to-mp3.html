How This Tool Works

Your browser decodes the M4A file using its built-in audio engine, then re-encodes the raw audio into MP3 format using an in-browser encoder. The entire process happens on your own device — your audio file is never uploaded anywhere, which keeps it completely private.

Because everything runs locally, larger files or longer recordings may take a little longer to convert, especially on older devices. A progress bar will show you how the conversion is going.

Choosing a Bitrate

Bitrate controls the balance between file size and sound quality. 128 kbps is good enough for voice recordings and podcasts. 192 kbps is a solid middle ground for most music. 320 kbps gives the best audio quality but results in a larger file.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why convert M4A to MP3?

MP3 is one of the most widely supported audio formats, playable on virtually every device and app, while M4A (common on Apple devices) isn't always supported elsewhere.

Is my audio file uploaded to a server?

No. The entire conversion happens inside your browser on your own device. Nothing is uploaded.

Is there a file size or length limit?

There's no strict limit, but very long audio files will take more time and memory to process since your device is doing all the work.
