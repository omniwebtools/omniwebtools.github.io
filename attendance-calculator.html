How This Calculator Works

Most schools and colleges require students to attend a minimum percentage of classes — often 75%. Falling below this can affect exam eligibility or grades. This tool helps you understand exactly where you stand.

Enter the total number of classes held so far and how many you actually attended. The calculator instantly shows your current attendance percentage.

If you're below your required percentage, the tool tells you how many classes in a row you need to attend to get back on track. If you're above it, the tool tells you how many classes you can still afford to miss without dropping below the requirement.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a typical attendance requirement?

It varies by institution, but 75% is one of the most common minimums used by schools and colleges.

Why do I need to enter "classes remaining"?

This is optional — it helps the calculator give you a more accurate answer if you already know how many classes are left in the term, since it can factor future classes into the calculation.

Is my data saved anywhere?

No. All calculations happen instantly in your browser. Nothing is sent to a server.
